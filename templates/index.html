Our Story

Katie and Carlos first met in the summer of 2012 when they ended up subletting the same apartment in Ithaca, NY for summer classes. Katie was relieved when Carlos short term sublet was up and he moved out, leaving her to have the apartment for herself for the remainder of the summer. However, weeks before their fall co-op internship in Palo Alto, CA began they were notified that they would be sharing an apartment again for the duration of the fall internship.

They started dating, even as Katie spent the following spring semester studying abroad in Barcelona. Throughout the next summer back in Palo Alto together, and the following two years at Cornell, their relationship flourished.

After Cornell, Katie and Carlos spent a year in a long-distance relationship, with Katie in Boston, MA and Carlos in Charlotte, NC. Katie then moved to Charlotte, where they spent the next 4 years developing their careers and more importantly, their partnership together.

On May 30, 2021, We Said Yes!

Getting to Cartagena

Airport

We recommend arriving by plane to Cartagena's Rafael Núñez International Airport (CTG), which is located about a 15 minute taxi ride from all of the recommended hotels. From the U.S., there are non-stop flights from Miami (MIA), Fort Lauderdale (FLL), and New York (JFK). Layovers in Miami (MIA), Bogotá (BOG), Medellín (MDE), or Panamá (PTY) are common for one-stop itineraries from the United States or Europe.

Airlines

In addition to well-known U.S. and European airlines, other reliable companies serving regular commercial flights to the Cartagena airport (CTG) include:

  • Avianca (Colombia's flag carrier)
  • Copa Airlines (Panama's flag carrier)
  • LATAM Airlines

Low-cost carriers Spirit Airlines and Viva Air also fly into Cartagena. However, due to reliability issues, we do not recommend booking through these airlines if other alternatives exist.

Travel Tips

Make a plan for how you will be using your phone to communicate with your fellow travelers and friends and family back home. If you are planning on relying on Wi-Fi, confirm your device's Wi-Fi calling and texting capabilities. If you are planning on utilizing an international phone plan, enable the plan before your departure and ensure recurring services and charges beyond the dates for your travel are disabled.

You do not need a rental car - taxis are safe, cheap, and plentiful. Be conscientious about getting in a taxi at an official stand, the Santa Clara has a taxi stand right in front of it and several other hotels do as well. When in doubt, hotel staff at any hotel can help you arrange a ride. Screenshot common destinations (your hotel, Sofitel Santa Clara) in advance to facilitate communication with taxi drivers.

We recommend downloading Spanish in Google Translate before your trip. Many people in Cartagena speak some English, but not everyone. Google Translate is a common tool that Cartageneros will use to make sure both parties understand the conversation at hand.

We recommend downloading an offline map of Cartagena in Google Maps to your mobile device before your trip. This will ensure your ability to navigate the colonial-era streets of historic Cartagena regardless of your international phone plan.

If possible, procure some Colombian pesos (COP) before you arrive in Cartagena. This may be best accomplished through ordering them through your bank or stopping at a currency exchange in your home city. We do not recommend airport currency exchanges in the US or Colombia as they typically charge high fees and provide unfavorable exchange rates. Confirm with your bank and credit card what sorts of fees you may have added to credit card transactions or ATM withdrawals to avoid surprises on your statement when you return.

We are thrilled that you are planning on joining us in Cartagena. Please exercise good, common sense as a traveller in a new city. Just like any big city or heavy tourist traffic - be aware of your personal effects when you are out and about, you should feel comfortable using your devices but don't flaunt them, and do not purchase food or drinks from street vendors (there are some family stories about the effects of consuming street ceviche that serve as fair warning). There are many street vendors in Cartagena and Katie and Carlos purchased items from these vendors that are still household treasures to this day. However, you should never feel pressured to purchase anything or give anyone money, when in doubt just walk away.

Cartagena Highlights

Cartagena is a beautiful city with a rich, complex history. Lonely Planet has an excellent, comprehensive page for international tourists visiting Cartagena here: https://www.lonelyplanet.com/colombia/caribbean-coast/cartagena. We would especially recommend touring Castillo de San Felipe de Barajas, one the most impressive colonial forts of the Spanish colonial era in excellent condition.

Condé Nast Traveller also provides great dining and shopping inspiration here: https://www.cntraveller.in/story/cartagena-is-shining-brighter-than-ever. We would especially recommend dining at Carmen for dinner and lunch or merienda (a light afternoon snack) at La Cevicheria. The linked article spends some time recommending the Gethsemane neighborhood outside of Cartagena's walls, this is not a recommendation we can fully endorse, please use caution and common sense as you are touring Cartagena. There are wonderful and varied sites to see and things to do in the historic, walled city.

If you are looking to spend some time lounging we recommend inquiring with your hotel spa for available treatment options before your arrival or plan on luxuriating over a midday meal in town that lets you sample the delicious local cuisine and people-watch over your beverage(s) of choice. The beaches immediately around Cartagena are not set up for traditional beach lounging. If you are able to join us on Wednesday for our island beach excursion, we would love to have you! Otherwise, you may want to plan on lounging pool-side at your hotel.
